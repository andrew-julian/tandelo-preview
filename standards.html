Content Standards

The Tandelo Standard

Every article placed through Tandelo goes through editorial review. These standards aren't just rules — they're what makes our network worth placing content on. Real readers, real trust, real value.

✅
What we accept
  • ✓ Original, well-written content with genuine informational or promotional value
  • ✓ Content that has been human-edited (pure AI generation without editing is not accepted)
  • ✓ Promotional content that doesn't exploit fear, harm, or vulnerable groups
  • ✓ Topics that align with a quality news and information environment
  • ✓ Up to 4 do-follow links, up to 4 images (with credited sources)
  • ✓ Maximum 2,000 words (longer articles available as an add-on)
  • ✓ Sensitive topics handled with neutral language, clear informational purpose, and appropriate disclaimers
✗
What we don't accept
  • ✗ Tobacco, alcohol, firearms, illegal activity, violence, pornography
  • ✗ Hate speech — including sexist, racist, ableist, discriminatory, or homophobic content
  • ✗ Content that exploits harm, fear, tragedy, or real-world events to drive commercial outcomes
  • ✗ Links to exploitative, deceptive, or unethical services
  • ✗ Pure AI-generated output with no human editing
  • ✗ Tools or guides that enable deception, platform evasion, or circumvention of detection systems
  • ✗ Content that presents commercial legal or medical services as editorial authority without appropriate disclaimers
⚠
Handle with care

The following topics require extra care. They're not automatically declined, but they get closer editorial scrutiny. We may ask for additional information about intended use, safeguards, or regulatory compliance.

  • ⚠ AI products and emerging technologies — we verify the tool's purpose and that it's used ethically
  • ⚠ Geopolitically sensitive regions or subjects
  • ⚠ Services with higher misuse risk (privacy tools, identity services, surveillance)
  • ⚠ Legal advice framing — acceptable with disclaimers, not acceptable as editorial authority for a commercial law firm
  • ⚠ Medical or health advice that goes beyond general information into specific treatment recommendations

Technical specs

  • Max 2,000 words
  • Max 4 do-follow links
  • Max 4 images (source required)
  • Human-edited content only
  • No markdown required — plain text accepted

On publication

  • Article goes live as-is (we don't rewrite)
  • Marked as sponsored content
  • Do-follow links preserved
  • Auto-submitted for indexing
  • Permanent — no expiry

Category changes

If we determine your article falls into a different category than you selected, we always confirm with you before proceeding. You can revise or cancel — no charge until you approve.

Our approach

How we review every article

We look beyond the article itself. Context, framing, timing, and the service being promoted all matter.

1
The topic itself

Is the subject matter something we'd be comfortable placing on our network, regardless of how it's framed? Some topics are simply out of scope.

2
Framing and tone

Factually accurate content can still be declined if the framing is exploitative, misleading, or designed to create fear or urgency around a vulnerable situation.

3
The promoted product or service

What is the article ultimately promoting? We review the linked site independently. A well-written article can be declined purely because of where its links lead.

4
Reader interpretation

How would a reasonable reader interpret this content? Is the commercial purpose clear? Does the article respect the reader's intelligence and trust?

About declines

A decline doesn't mean your content is wrong or that your business is problematic — it means it doesn't fit our network at this time.

Where possible, we'll explain why and offer a path forward: revised framing, a different link, or a different article angle that would be acceptable.

Talk to us before submitting
  • We always confirm category changes before invoicing
  • We explain declines where we can
  • We suggest revisions when a fix is possible
  • We reserve the right to remove live articles if later review or complaints show they no longer meet our standards
  • A decline on one article doesn't close the door on future submissions
