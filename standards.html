Content Standards

The Tandelo Standard

Every article placed through Tandelo goes through editorial review. These standards protect readers, maintain the value of our network, and keep your content in a quality environment.

✅ What we accept

  • ✓ Original, well-written content with genuine informational or promotional value
  • ✓ Content that has been meaningfully human-edited (pure AI generation without editing is not accepted)
  • ✓ Promotional content that doesn't exploit fear, harm, or vulnerable groups
  • ✓ Topics that fit a quality news and information environment
  • ✓ Up to 4 do-follow links, up to 4 images with credited sources
  • ✓ Maximum 2,000 words (extensions available as add-on)
  • ✓ Sensitive topics handled with neutral language, clear purpose, and appropriate disclaimers

✗ What we don't accept

  • ✗ Tobacco, alcohol, firearms, illegal activity, violence, pornography
  • ✗ Hate speech — including sexist, racist, ableist, discriminatory, or homophobic content
  • ✗ Content that exploits harm, fear, tragedy, or real-world events to drive commercial outcomes
  • ✗ Links to exploitative, deceptive, or unethical services
  • ✗ Pure AI-generated output with no human editing
  • ✗ Tools or guides enabling deception, platform evasion, or circumvention of detection systems
  • ✗ Content presenting commercial legal or medical services as editorial authority without disclaimers
  • ✗ "Mail order brides" or any content commodifying people from vulnerable groups

⚠ Handle with care

Not automatically declined, but these get closer scrutiny. We may ask for additional context about intended use, safeguards, or regulatory compliance.

  • ⚠ AI products and emerging technologies — we verify purpose and that they're used ethically
  • ⚠ Geopolitically sensitive regions or subjects
  • ⚠ Services with higher misuse risk (privacy tools, identity services, surveillance)
  • ⚠ Legal advice framing — acceptable with disclaimers; not acceptable as editorial authority for a commercial law firm
  • ⚠ Medical or health advice that goes beyond general information into specific treatment recommendations
  • ⚠ TikTok downloaders and similar tools — reviewed on a case-by-case basis including independent site check

Our approach

How we review every article

We look beyond the article itself. Context, framing, timing, and the service being promoted all matter.

1

The topic itself

Is the subject matter something we'd be comfortable placing on our network, regardless of framing? Some topics are simply out of scope.

2

Framing and tone

Factually accurate content can still be declined if the framing is exploitative, misleading, or designed to create fear or urgency around a sensitive situation.

3

The promoted product or service

What is the article ultimately promoting? We review the linked site independently. A well-written article can be declined purely because of where its links lead.

4

Reader interpretation

How would a reasonable reader interpret this content? Is the commercial purpose clear? Does the article respect the reader's intelligence and trust?

In practice

Generally acceptable vs. likely declined

The same topic can be acceptable or declined depending on framing. Here's how we think about borderline cases.

✓ Generally acceptable
  • Explaining trends, research, or policy in safety, health, or technology with neutral language
  • Discussing difficult issues with clear informational purpose and no commercial hook
  • Promoting products and services without relying on fear, urgency, or real-world harm
  • AI tools that help humans write more authentically (detection tools, editing assistants)
  • General HR or workplace management content with no commercial law firm named
  • Sensitive topics with disclaimers, neutral framing, and no specific brand recommendation
✗ Likely declined
  • Leading with a recent violent or tragic event to justify a commercial product or service
  • Using emotionally charged language to create urgency or fear around a sensitive situation
  • Linking to services that commodify, exploit, or misrepresent people or sensitive groups
  • Normalising tools that enable deception, evasion, or unethical behaviour
  • Articles citing or quoting commercial law firms as authorities in sensitive legal contexts
  • AI tools that help content evade detection (the opposite of writing authentically)

About declines

A decline doesn't mean your content is wrong or your business is problematic — it means it doesn't fit our network at this time.

Where possible, we'll explain why and offer a path forward — a different angle, link change, or revised framing that would be acceptable.

Talk to us before submitting
  • We always confirm category changes before invoicing
  • We explain declines where possible and suggest revisions when a fix exists
  • A decline on one article doesn't close the door on future submissions
  • We reserve the right to remove live articles if later review or complaints show they no longer meet our standards
  • We do not write or rewrite articles — that's your job, we're here to place them
